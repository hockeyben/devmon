# The World of DevMon

DevMon takes place in a dev-world laid over the terminal you already live in —
five regions, each one a stretch of the software stack made literal, each
home to its own roster of wild DevMon. You don't travel here by choice so
much as by leveling up: every region is gated by how far your real coding
work has carried you.

## Termina Meadows (levels 1-15)

Rolling starter fields where every developer's journey begins. The grass is
soft, the stakes are low, and the DevMon here — hex owlets, ember kits,
byte hares — are as gentle an introduction to the wild as the stack gets.
This is where you learn to read a diff of a battle: what wins, what heals,
what runs. Nobody remembers Termina Meadows fondly for its difficulty; they
remember it for being where they started.

## Compiler Wastes (levels 15-30)

Past the meadows the ground cracks into an industrial badland: overheating
build servers, rusted-out toolchains, slag heaps of failed compiles. The
DevMon that live here — forge wyrms, rust mites, crucible titans — are
tougher and hotter-tempered than anything in Termina Meadows, forged (some
of them literally) in the heat of a thousand red CI runs. This is where the
game stops being an on-ramp and starts being a fight.

## Cloud Reaches (levels 30-50)

Altitude changes everything. Cloud Reaches is windswept and unstable,
availability zones drifting like weather fronts, static crackling between
regions that might not be there tomorrow. Storm raptors and nimbus hawks
patrol the updrafts; barometer bats read the pressure before anyone else
knows it's changing. Trainers who make it this far have learned that uptime
is never guaranteed — you build for the storm, not around it.

## Kernel Depths (levels 50-70)

Below user-space, below the operating system itself, is a dark and
cave-like substrate of raw memory and bare metal. Kernel Depths is where
DevMon stop resembling anything friendly — segfault ghouls, phantom
daemons, dread processes drifting through corridors that were never meant
to be walked by anything with a stack trace. Light is scarce. So is mercy.
Whatever broke down here stayed broken, and it's still hungry.

## The Voidnet (levels 70-95)

Beyond the edge of the routing table, past the last documented endpoint,
lies the Voidnet — the eldritch endgame, mostly undocumented, largely
unmapped, and home to the legendaries. Recursion hydras loop without base
cases. Kernel panic wyrms leave crash dumps in their wake. Singulon is
rumored rather than confirmed. Nobody arrives at the Voidnet by accident;
you earn every level it takes to survive here, and even then it doesn't
promise you'll leave with everything you brought in.
